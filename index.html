Sydney Opera House

Opera House, 21 Circular Quay in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. One of the most famous and easily recognizable buildings in the world, a symbol and "visiting card" of the largest city in the country, as well as one of the main tourist attractions of the continent. This building is an internationally recognized masterpiece of Australian and world architecture. It has a unique series of sail-shaped shells that form the roof, which makes the theater unlike any other building in the world. On June 28, 2007, it was listed by UNESCO as a World Heritage Site, and is the only building from the 1970s with this status. In addition, the building was a candidate for inclusion in the list of "New Seven Wonders of the World", but lost in the final.

Notre-Dame de Paris

Notre-Dame de Paris is a Catholic church in the center of Paris, one of the symbols of the French capital. Cathedral of the Archdiocese of Paris. One of the first large Gothic cathedrals with a six-part ribbed vault, while retaining the characteristics of the transition from the Romanesque style of Normandy: double side longitudinal naves and the presence of stands (upper galleries). The portal of St. Anne on the right is the oldest (end of the 12th century); the portal of the Last Judgment in the center (1220-1230) has undergone many restorations; The portal of the Mother of God is north, on the left (beginning of the 13th century).

Taj Mahal

The mausoleum-mosque, located in Agra, India, on the banks of the Jamna River. Built by order of the descendant of Tamerlane, the padishah. The Taj Mahal is considered the finest example of Mughal architecture, which combines elements of Indian, Persian and Arab architectural styles. Inside the mausoleum there are two tombs - the shah and his wife. The walls are lined with polished translucent marble inlaid with gems. Turquoise, agate, malachite, carnelian, etc. were used.

Big Ben

Big Ben is a popular tourist name for the clock tower of the Palace of Westminster. Originally "Big Ben" was the name of the largest of the six bells. At the time of its casting, Big Ben was the largest and heaviest bell in the United Kingdom. The Elizabeth Tower is one of the most recognizable symbols of Great Britain, often used in souvenirs, advertisements, and movies. It is believed that the bell was named Big Ben in honor of Sir Benjamin Hall, the supervisor of the construction works. According to another version, he is named after Benjamin Count, a famous heavyweight boxer.

Colosseum

The Colosseum or Flavian Amphitheater is an amphitheater, an architectural monument of Ancient Rome. The construction of the largest amphitheater of the ancient world, with a capacity of over 50 thousand people, was carried out for eight years as a collective building of the emperors of the Flavian dynasty. The history of the Colosseum dates back to 68, when the treason of the Praetorian Guard and the condemnation of the Senate forced Emperor Nero, after fourteen years of despotic rule of the state, to commit suicide in a country villa near Rome.

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a metal tower in the center of Paris, its most recognizable architectural landmark. The tower was built in 1889 and was originally conceived as a temporary structure serving as the entrance arch of the 1889 Paris World's Fair. The Eiffel Tower is called the most visited paid and most photographed attraction in the world. The tower is available for visits every day. On the first and second floors of the tower there are buffets and a restaurant. On the 2nd floor there is a more expensive establishment - the Le Jules Verne restaurant. The third floor at the top of the tower is occupied by the Champagne Bar.

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is a city of ancient America, located on the territory of modern Peru at an altitude of 2400 meters above sea level. In 2007 he was awarded the title of New Wonder of the World. Also Machu Picchu is often called "the city in ruins" or "the city among the clouds", also sometimes called the "lost city of the Incas". Built by Pachacutec as an imperial residence, as well as to perpetuate his name in history, Machu Picchu was the third such sanctuary residence. For more than 400 years this city was forgotten and was in desolation. He was discovered by an American researcher Professor Hiram Bingham.

Louvre

One of the largest and most popular art museums in the world. The museum is located in the center of Paris. The museum building is an old royal palace, one of the oldest museums with a rich history of collecting art and historical relics in France, from the Capetian dynasty to the present day. His collections cover vast geographic and temporal spaces: from western Europe to Iran through Greece, Egypt and the Middle East; from antiquity to 1848. The idea to create a museum in the Louvre to display masterpieces from the royal collections appeared even before the French Revolution.

Great Sphinx of Giza

On the western bank of the Nile in Giza - the oldest surviving monumental sculpture on Earth, It was carved from a monolithic limestone rock in the form of a colossal sphinx - a lion lying on the sand, whose face, as has long been considered, is given a portrait resemblance to the pharaoh Khefren. The statue is 73 meters long and 20 meters high; between the forepaws was a small sanctuary. The statue is located facing the Nile and the rising sun. Almost all ancient Eastern civilizations saw in a lion a symbol of a solar deity. Since ancient times, the pharaoh has been portrayed as a lion destroying his enemies.

Great Wall of China

The dividing wall is almost 9000 km long, built in ancient China. The largest architectural monument. The construction of the first sections of the wall began in the 3rd century BC. NS. during the period of the Warring States to protect the state from the Xiongnu. The construction was attended by a fifth of the then population of the country, that is, about a million people. The settlements developing on the plain of central China, turning into large centers of trade, attracted the attention of the nomads, who often attacked them, making raids from beyond Yinshan.
